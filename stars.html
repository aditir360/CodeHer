CodeLibrary

Code Stars is a showcase of exceptional women in technology, highlighting their achievements, stories, and contributions to the tech industry. By sharing their experiences and successes, Code Stars aims to inspire and empower others, promoting diversity, inclusion, and gender equality in the world of coding and computer science.

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace was a British mathematician and writer who is often considered the world's first computer programmer, known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is considered to be the first computer program, making her a pioneer in the field of computer science and a trailblazer for women in technology.

Radia Perlman

Radia Perlman, known as the "Mother of the Internet," is an American computer scientist and network engineer who developed the spanning tree protocol, an algorithm that enables Ethernet networks to function efficiently and reliably. Her groundbreaking work has had a profound impact on the development of network bridges, routers, and switches, and has enabled the scalable and robust networking infrastructure that underlies the modern internet.

Sally Floyd

Sally Floyd, a computer scientist and network engineer, made significant contributions to the development of the Internet, including the design of TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) and the development of protocols for congestion control. Her work has had a lasting impact on the stability and reliability of the Internet.



Grace Hopper

Grace Hopper, an American computer scientist and Navy Rear Admiral, developed the first compiler and was a key figure in the development of COBOL, a high-level programming language. Her work revolutionized computer programming, making it more accessible and user-friendly, and she became a pioneer for women in technology.

Mary Lou Jepsen

Mary Lou Jepsen, a computer scientist and inventor, developed the display technology for the original Xbox and is a pioneer in the field of display technology. She has founded several companies, including Pixel Qi, which developed low-power displays for laptops and other devices.

Marissa Mayer

Marissa Mayer, a computer scientist and business executive, was the first female engineer at Google and later became the CEO of Yahoo! She has been a pioneer for women in technology and a role model for women in leadership positions in the tech industry.
